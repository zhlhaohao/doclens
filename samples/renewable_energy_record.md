# Global Renewable Energy Records: 2025 Annual Review

## A Transformative Year for Clean Energy

The year 2025 has set new benchmarks across virtually every metric in the global renewable energy sector. From installation capacity to investment volumes to technological breakthroughs, the clean energy transition accelerated at an unprecedented pace.

## Market Overview

### Investment
Global renewable energy investment reached $1.2 trillion in 2025, a 23% increase over 2024. The compound annual growth rate (CAGR) for renewable energy investment over the past five years stands at 12.3%, significantly outpacing fossil fuel investment growth of 2.1%.

### Share of Electricity Generation
Renewable sources accounted for 43% of global electricity generation in 2025, up from 39% in 2024. The International Energy Agency (IEA) now projects that renewables will surpass 50% of global electricity generation by 2028, five years earlier than previously estimated.

### Total Installed Capacity
Global renewable energy capacity exceeded 5,000 GW for the first time:

- **Solar PV**: 2,200 GW (+35% year-over-year)
- **Wind**: 1,200 GW (+18% year-over-year)
- **Hydropower**: 1,400 GW (+2% year-over-year)
- **Other (biomass, geothermal, marine)**: 200 GW (+8% year-over-year)

## Solar Photovoltaic Records

### Installation Records
Solar PV continues to be the fastest-growing energy technology in human history:

- **Annual installations**: 650 GW added in 2025 (vs. 480 GW in 2024)
- **China alone**: 318 GW of new solar installations, accounting for 49% of global additions
- **India**: 45 GW added, making it the third-largest solar market
- **United States**: 55 GW added, driven by Inflation Reduction Act incentives
- **European Union**: 75 GW added, led by Germany (18 GW) and Spain (12 GW)

### Technology Breakthroughs
Several solar technology records fell in 2025:

- **Perovskite-silicon tandem cell efficiency**: 33.9% ( LONGi Green Energy, confirmed by NREL)
- **Perovskite single-junction**: 26.1% (UNIST, South Korea)
- **Commercial module efficiency**: 24.5% average for TOPCon technology
- **Bifacial module gain**: 12-15% additional energy yield confirmed in large-scale deployments

### Cost Reductions
Solar PV module prices continued their dramatic descent:

- **Average module price**: $0.10/Watt (vs. $0.16/Watt in 2024)
- **System LCOE**: $20-30/MWh for utility-scale projects in optimal locations
- **Lowest bid recorded**: $13.5/MWh in Saudi Arabia (1.5 GW project)

At these price levels, solar PV is the cheapest source of new electricity generation in countries representing over 85% of global GDP.

## Wind Energy Records

### Offshore Wind
Offshore wind had a record year with 25 GW of new installations:

- **China**: 12 GW added, cumulative capacity exceeding 60 GW
- **United Kingdom**: 3.5 GW added, including the first phase of Dogger Bank (3.6 GW total)
- **South Korea**: 1.2 GW added, entering the top 5 offshore markets
- **Floating offshore wind**: 500 MW installed globally, with projects in Portugal, France, and China

### Turbine Technology
Turbine size records continued to be broken:

- **Largest operational turbine**: Goldwind GWH-252-16.0 (16 MW, 252-meter rotor diameter)
- **Largest prototype**: CSSC H260-18.0 (18 MW, 260-meter rotor diameter)
- **Trend**: Average new offshore turbine size exceeded 12 MW in 2025

### Onshore Wind
- **Global additions**: 100 GW of new onshore wind capacity
- **Lowest LCOE**: $22/MWh in Brazil and the United States (Great Plains)
- **Repowering trend**: 15 GW of old turbines replaced with modern, higher-capacity units

## Energy Storage

### Battery Storage
Battery energy storage systems (BESS) saw explosive growth:

- **Annual installations**: 120 GWh added globally (vs. 75 GWh in 2024)
- **China**: 60 GWh added, driven by mandatory storage requirements for renewable projects
- **United States**: 30 GWh added, California and Texas leading
- **Battery costs**: $100/kWh at cell level, $150/kWh at system level for 4-hour duration
- **Long-duration storage**: Iron-air and flow battery technologies reached commercial scale with 100+ GWh pipeline

### Pumped Hydro
- **New additions**: 10 GW globally
- **Notable projects**: Fengning Pumped Storage (3.6 GW) in China became the world's largest

## Regional Highlights

### China
China continued to dominate across all renewable energy metrics:

- Renewable capacity exceeded 1,840 GW (surpassing thermal power capacity for the first time)
- Solar PV manufacturing: 85% of global module production
- Wind turbine exports: 25 GW exported to 50+ countries
- New energy storage: 100 GW/300 GWh deployed

### Europe
The European Union accelerated its energy transition:

- Renewable share of electricity: 52% (first time above 50%)
- Solar PV: Every EU member state now generates at least 5% of electricity from solar
- Offshore wind: North Sea Energy Cooperation agreement targeting 120 GW by 2030

### United States
The Inflation Reduction Act continued to drive massive investment:

- Clean energy investment: $140 billion (up from $95 billion in 2024)
- Solar manufacturing: 40 GW of domestic module capacity came online
- Grid-scale storage: Pipeline exceeded 400 GWh

## Outlook for 2026-2030

The trajectory is clear: renewable energy is on track to become the dominant source of global electricity by the end of this decade. Key projections include:

- **2026**: Global renewable capacity to exceed 6,000 GW
- **2028**: Renewables to surpass 50% of global electricity generation
- **2030**: Solar PV alone may exceed 4,000 GW cumulative capacity
- **2030**: Battery storage costs projected to fall below $80/kWh

The remaining challenges — grid integration, permitting bottlenecks, and supply chain diversification — are significant but increasingly being addressed through policy reform and technological innovation.
