# Marathon Training Guide: From Couch to 26.2 Miles

## The Marathon Challenge

Completing a marathon (42.195 kilometers / 26.2 miles) is one of the most rewarding physical challenges a person can undertake. With proper preparation, approximately 95% of healthy individuals who follow a structured training plan can complete the distance. This guide provides a comprehensive 16-week training plan and the knowledge you need to cross the finish line.

## Prerequisites

Before starting a marathon training plan, you should be able to:
- Run continuously for 30 minutes without stopping
- Comfortably complete a 10K (6.2 miles) race
- Have been running consistently for at least 6 months
- Have medical clearance from your physician

## The 16-Week Training Plan

### Phase 1: Base Building (Weeks 1-4)
Build aerobic endurance and establish the training habit.

| Day | Workout | Duration/Pace |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Monday | Rest or cross-training | - |
| Tuesday | Easy run | 30-40 min, conversational pace |
| Wednesday | Cross-training or rest | 30 min (swimming, cycling, yoga) |
| Thursday | Easy run | 30-40 min |
| Friday | Rest | - |
| Saturday | Long run | 8-10 miles, very easy pace |
| Sunday | Recovery walk or cross-training | 30 min |

Weekly mileage: 15-20 miles

### Phase 2: Endurance Building (Weeks 5-8)
Increase long run distance and introduce speed work.

| Day | Workout | Duration/Pace |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Monday | Rest | - |
| Tuesday | Tempo run | 15 min warmup + 20 min tempo + 10 min cooldown |
| Wednesday | Easy run | 40-50 min |
| Thursday | Interval training | 6-8 x 800m at 5K pace, 400m recovery jog |
| Friday | Rest | - |
| Saturday | Long run | 12-15 miles |
| Sunday | Easy recovery run | 20-30 min |

Weekly mileage: 25-32 miles

### Phase 3: Peak Training (Weeks 9-13)
The most demanding phase with the longest runs.

| Day | Workout | Duration/Pace |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Monday | Rest | - |
| Tuesday | Tempo run | 15 min warmup + 30 min tempo + 10 min cooldown |
| Wednesday | Easy run | 45-60 min |
| Thursday | Speed work | 4-6 x 1 mile at half marathon pace, 800m recovery |
| Friday | Easy run | 30-40 min |
| Saturday | Long run | 16-20 miles (peak at week 11: 20 miles) |
| Sunday | Recovery run or cross-training | 20-30 min |

Weekly mileage: 35-45 miles

### Phase 4: Taper (Weeks 14-16)
Reduce volume while maintaining intensity to allow your body to recover and store energy.

| Week | Long Run | Weekly Mileage | Key Focus |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| 14 | 14 miles | 30 miles | Reduce volume by 25-30% |
| 15 | 10 miles | 20 miles | Reduce volume by 50% |
| 16 | Race week! | 5-8 miles pre-race | Stay loose, carb load |

## Pace Strategy

### Calculating Your Target Pace
Use a recent race result (10K or half marathon) to estimate your marathon pace:

| Recent Half Marathon | Estimated Marathon | Marathon Pace (min/mile) |
|---------------------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| 1:30:00 | 3:10:00 | 7:15 |
| 1:45:00 | 3:42:00 | 8:28 |
| 2:00:00 | 4:15:00 | 9:43 |
| 2:15:00 | 4:48:00 | 10:58 |
| 2:30:00 | 5:20:00 | 12:12 |

### Race Day Pace Strategy
The most common mistake is starting too fast. Follow this strategy:

- **Miles 1-5**: 10-15 seconds per mile SLOWER than goal pace
- **Miles 6-13**: Settle into goal pace
- **Miles 14-20**: Maintain goal pace, this is where the mental challenge begins
- **Miles 21-26.2**: Give whatever you have left

Starting 10 seconds per mile too fast in the first 5 miles can cost you 30-60 seconds per mile in the final 6 miles.

## Nutrition and Hydration

### During Training
- **Daily carbohydrate intake**: 5-7g per kg of body weight
- **Daily protein**: 1.4-1.8g per kg of body weight
- **Long run fueling**: Consume 30-60g of carbohydrate per hour after the first 60 minutes
- **Hydration**: Drink 400-800ml per hour of running, adjusted for weather conditions

### Race Day Nutrition
- **Pre-race meal** (3-4 hours before): 100-150g carbohydrate, low fiber, low fat (bagel with jam, banana, oatmeal)
- **During the race**: Energy gel every 40-45 minutes (6-8 gels total)
- **Hydration**: Drink at every aid station (alternating water and sports drink)

### Carb Loading (Week 16)
The 3-day carb loading protocol:
- **Day 1 (Wednesday)**: 8-10g carbs per kg body weight, reduce training
- **Day 2 (Thursday)**: 8-10g carbs per kg, very light training
- **Day 3 (Friday)**: 8-10g carbs per kg, rest day
- Avoid high-fiber and high-fat foods during carb loading
- This increases glycogen stores by 50-100%, providing fuel for the full distance

## Recovery Strategies

### Post-Run Recovery
1. **Within 30 minutes**: Consume 30-50g carbohydrate + 15-25g protein (chocolate milk, protein shake with banana)
2. **Hydrate**: Drink 500-750ml of fluid for every pound lost during the run
3. **Stretching**: 10-15 minutes of gentle static stretching
4. **Foam rolling**: 5-10 minutes targeting quads, hamstrings, calves, and IT band
5. **Epsom salt bath**: 2 cups of Epsom salts in warm water for 15-20 minutes

### Sleep
Adequate sleep is the single most important recovery tool:
- Aim for 7-9 hours per night during training
- During peak training weeks, consider a 20-30 minute afternoon nap
- Good sleep hygiene: consistent bedtime, dark room, no screens 1 hour before bed

### Injury Prevention
Common running injuries and prevention:

| Injury | Symptoms | Prevention |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Runner's knee | Pain around kneecap | Strengthen quads and glutes |
| IT band syndrome | Outer knee/hip pain | Foam rolling, hip abductor exercises |
| Plantar fasciitis | Heel/arch pain | Calf stretching, proper footwear |
| Shin splints | Lower leg pain | Gradual mileage increase, proper shoes |
| Achilles tendinitis | Back of heel pain | Eccentric calf raises, proper warmup |

### The 10% Rule
Never increase your weekly mileage by more than 10% from the previous week. This single rule prevents the majority of overuse injuries.

## Race Day Checklist

- Race bib and safety pins
- Timing chip (if separate)
- Race outfit (tested on a long training run, never wear new gear on race day)
- Running shoes (with 100-300 miles on them, not brand new)
- Energy gels (6-8)
- Body glide or anti-chafe balm
- Throwaway clothes for the start line (stay warm before the race)
- Post-race change of clothes and towel
- Ibuprofen (if needed post-race)
