# Healthy Meal Prep Guide: Plan, Cook, and Eat Well

## Why Meal Prep Matters

Meal preparation — cooking meals or components in advance — is one of the most effective strategies for maintaining a healthy diet while saving time and money. Research published in the International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity found that people who meal prep are 50% more likely to consume the recommended daily servings of fruits and vegetables and 30% less likely to be overweight.

### Benefits at a Glance
- **Time savings**: 3-5 hours of cooking per week replaces daily 45-minute cooking sessions
- **Cost savings**: Home-cooked meal prep costs $3-7 per meal vs. $12-20 for restaurant meals
- **Portion control**: Pre-measured servings prevent overeating
- **Reduced food waste**: Strategic shopping uses 85-95% of purchased ingredients
- **Healthier choices**: Having healthy meals ready removes the temptation of convenience foods

## The Meal Prep Framework

### Step 1: Plan (Sunday or your preferred start day)

**Choose your prep style:**
- **Batch cooking**: Cook 2-3 large recipes and portion them out
- **Ingredient prep**: Chop, marinate, and prepare ingredients for quick assembly
- **Individual meals**: Cook complete meals in separate containers

**Plan for 5 days:**
- 5 lunches (work meals)
- 5 dinners
- 5-7 breakfasts (if desired)
- 10-15 snacks

### Step 2: Shop Efficiently

Create a consolidated shopping list organized by grocery store section:

```
Produce:
- 2 lbs chicken breast
- 1 head broccoli
- 2 bell peppers
- 1 bag spinach
- 4 sweet potatoes
- 2 onions, 4 garlic cloves
- 3 lemons

Grains & Legumes:
- 2 cups brown rice
- 1 can black beans
- 1 can chickpeas

Protein:
- 2 lbs chicken breast
- 1 dozen eggs
- 1 block firm tofu

Pantry:
- Olive oil, soy sauce, cumin, paprika
```

### Step 3: Cook Strategically

**Multi-task your cooking:**
1. Start long-cooking items first (rice, roasted vegetables, baked chicken)
2. While those cook, prepare stovetop items (sauteed vegetables, sauces)
3. Use waiting time for chopping and portioning
4. Cool all items to room temperature before refrigerating

## Sample Meal Plan (2,000 calories/day)

### Breakfast: Overnight Oats (Prep 5 jars)
Per jar:
- 1/2 cup rolled oats (150 cal)
- 1/2 cup Greek yogurt (100 cal)
- 1/2 cup milk (almond or dairy) (40 cal)
- 1 tbsp chia seeds (60 cal)
- 1/2 cup mixed berries (40 cal)
- 1 tbsp honey (optional) (65 cal)

Total per jar: approximately 455 calories, 20g protein, 8g fiber

### Lunch: Chicken Grain Bowls (Prep 5 containers)
Per container:
- 4 oz grilled chicken breast (185 cal, 35g protein)
- 1/2 cup cooked brown rice (110 cal)
- 1 cup roasted broccoli and bell peppers (80 cal)
- 1/4 cup chickpeas (80 cal)
- 2 tbsp lemon-tahini dressing (90 cal)

Total per container: approximately 545 calories, 42g protein, 10g fiber

### Dinner: Sheet Pan Salmon (Prep 2-3 servings)
Per serving:
- 5 oz baked salmon (290 cal, 28g protein)
- 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes (115 cal)
- 2 cups sauteed spinach with garlic (50 cal)
- 1 tbsp olive oil for cooking (120 cal)

Total per serving: approximately 575 calories, 33g protein, 8g fiber

### Snacks (Choose 2 per day)
- Apple + 1 tbsp almond butter (195 cal)
- 1/4 cup mixed nuts (175 cal)
- Carrot sticks + 2 tbsp hummus (130 cal)
- 1 hard-boiled egg (70 cal)
- Protein shake with 1 scoop whey (130 cal)

## Macronutrient Balance

For a balanced meal prep, aim for these ratios:

| Nutrient | Percentage of Calories | Daily Amount (2000 cal) |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Protein | 25-30% | 125-150g |
| Carbohydrates | 40-50% | 200-250g |
| Fat | 25-30% | 55-67g |
| Fiber | - | 25-30g minimum |

## Food Safety

Critical food safety guidelines for meal prep:

1. **Refrigerator storage**: Consume refrigerated meals within 4 days
2. **Cooling**: Cool cooked food to below 40F (4C) within 2 hours
3. **Reheating**: Heat to at least 165F (74C) internal temperature
4. **Cross-contamination**: Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and vegetables
5. **Container choice**: Use BPA-free, microwave-safe glass containers with tight-fitting lids

### Freezing Guidelines
These items freeze well for up to 3 months:
- Cooked grains (rice, quinoa)
- Soups and stews (leave 1 inch of space for expansion)
- Marinated raw proteins
- Muffins and breakfast burritos

These items do NOT freeze well:
- Raw vegetables (become mushy)
- Dairy-based sauces (separate)
- Fried foods (lose crispness)
- Hard-boiled eggs (rubbery texture)

## Weekly Schedule Template

**Sunday (2-3 hours):**
- Cook 2 cups brown rice in rice cooker
- Roast 2 lbs chicken breast at 425F for 20-25 minutes
- Roast broccoli and bell peppers (sheet pan, 20 minutes)
- Bake 4 sweet potatoes (45 minutes at 400F)
- Prepare 5 overnight oat jars
- Hard boil 6 eggs
- Make lemon-tahini dressing
- Portion all meals into containers

**Wednesday (20 minutes):**
- Prep Wednesday dinner (fresh salmon, quick-cooking)
- Check remaining meals and adjust if needed
- Refresh any wilted vegetables
